Introduction

Operating a business in Kenya requires compliance with various licensing requirements at both the county and national levels. The Single Business Permit (SBP) is the primary license required for most businesses, but many sectors also require additional industry-specific permits and approvals.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about business permits and licenses in Kenya, including costs by county, application procedures, renewal deadlines, and penalties for non-compliance.

📌 Did You Know?

Operating without a valid business permit can result in fines of up to KES 100,000 or closure of your business. Always ensure your permits are current.

Single Business Permit (SBP)

📋

Single Business Permit

County Government

The Single Business Permit is the primary license required for all businesses operating within a county. It consolidates multiple trading licenses into one permit.

Validity
1 Year
Processing Time
3-7 Days
Renewal
Annual

What it Covers:

  • General trading license
  • Operation of business premises
  • Advertising and signage
  • Food handling (basic)
  • Health inspection
  • Fire safety compliance

Application Process

1

Determine Required Permits

Identify all permits needed for your business type and location. Check with county and national authorities.

2

Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents (see next section). Ensure they're certified where necessary.

3

Visit County Offices or Portal

Most counties now have online portals. Alternatively, visit the County Licensing Office.

4

Submit Application

Complete application forms and submit with supporting documents.

5

Pay Fees

Pay prescribed fees via bank, M-Pesa, or e-citizen (depending on county).

6

Inspections

Some businesses require health, fire, or environmental inspections.

7

Receive Permit

Collect permit or download from portal once approved.

⚠️ Business Closure Risk

Counties conduct regular inspections and may close businesses operating without valid permits. Repeat offenders face prosecution.

Online Application Guide

Most counties now offer online permit applications through e-citizen:

  1. Login to e-citizen: ecitizen.go.ke
  2. Select your county: Choose from the list of counties
  3. Select permit type: Single Business Permit
  4. Fill application: Enter business details
  5. Upload documents: Attach required files (PDF format)
  6. Pay fees: Via M-Pesa, card, or bank
  7. Download permit: Print immediately upon approval

💡 Online Advantages

  • Apply anytime, anywhere
  • Track application status
  • Instant permit download
  • Payment receipts via email
  • Renewal reminders

Special Permits and Approvals

Liquor License

Required for businesses selling alcohol. Involves public participation and police clearance.

Cost: KES 10,000 - 100,000 depending on bar classification

Validity: 1 year

NEMA License

Environmental Impact Assessment license for manufacturing, construction, and other high-impact businesses.

Cost: KES 5,000 - 50,000 depending on project scale

Validity: Project-specific or annual

Fire Safety Certificate

Required for premises with public access. Issued after fire inspection.

Cost: KES 2,000 - 10,000

Validity: 1 year

Food Hygiene License

For food businesses. Requires health inspection and staff medical certificates.

Cost: KES 3,000 - 15,000

Validity: 1 year

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Wrong Business Classification

Misclassifying your business type can lead to incorrect fees and potential penalties.

❌ Missing Industry-Specific Licenses

SBP doesn't cover all permits. Many sectors need additional licenses.

❌ Operating in Wrong Zone

Some businesses (like bars) are restricted to certain zones.

❌ Late Renewal

Waiting until January means penalties and possible inspection delays.

❌ Not Displaying Permit

Permits must be prominently displayed at your business premises.

❌ Incorrect Business Address

Operating from a different address than on permit is an offense.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a permit if I work from home?

▼

Yes, home-based businesses still require a Single Business Permit. You'll need landlord's consent.

How long does the permit last?

▼

Single Business Permits are valid for one calendar year, expiring on 31st December.

Can I transfer my permit to another location?

▼

No, permits are location-specific. You need a new permit for a new address.

What if I operate in multiple counties?

▼

You need separate permits for each county where you have a physical presence.

Do online businesses need permits?

▼

Yes, if you have physical operations or inventory in Kenya, you need permits.

Can I get a refund if I close my business?

▼

Permit fees are generally non-refundable, even if you close mid-year.
