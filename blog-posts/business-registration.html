Introduction

Starting a business in Kenya has never been easier. With the introduction of the eCitizen portal and the Business Registration Service (BRS), you can now register your company entirely online without visiting any government office.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through every step of the business registration process, from choosing the right business structure to obtaining your CR12 certificate and KRA PIN. Whether you're a Kenyan resident or a foreign investor, this guide has you covered.

📌 Did You Know?

Kenya ranks among the top 5 countries in Africa for ease of doing business, with company registration taking as little as 24 hours when all documents are in order.

Company Name Search

Before registering, you must ensure your desired company name is available.

1

Login to eCitizen

Go to ecitizen.go.ke and login or create an account.

2

Navigate to Business Registration Service

Select "Business Registration Service" from the list of ministries.

3

Search Company Name

Enter your desired company name and search. Try multiple variations if your first choice is taken.

4

Reserve Name

If available, reserve the name immediately. Name reservation costs KES 150 and is valid for 30 days.

KRA PIN Registration

After incorporation, you must register your company for a KRA PIN:

  1. Visit iTax Portal: itax.kra.go.ke
  2. Register as a new taxpayer: Select "Company" as taxpayer type
  3. Enter incorporation details: Certificate number, registration date
  4. Provide director details: All directors' PINs and ID numbers
  5. Submit: PIN is issued instantly upon approval

Required for KRA PIN:

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • CR12 (list of directors)
  • Directors' KRA PINs
  • Registered office address

Business Permits & Licenses

Single Business Permit

All businesses operating in Kenya require a Single Business Permit from the county government where they operate.

County Typical Cost Validity
Nairobi City County KES 5,000 - 50,000 (based on sector) 1 year
Mombasa County KES 3,000 - 40,000 1 year
Kisumu County KES 2,500 - 30,000 1 year

Industry-Specific Licenses

  • Food businesses: Health license, KEBS certification
  • Transport: Transport Licensing Board permit
  • Tourism: Tourism Regulatory Authority license
  • Financial services: Central Bank or CMA license
  • Alcohol: County liquor license

Understanding CR12

What is CR12?

CR12 is the official document from the Business Registration Service showing the current directors and shareholders of a company. It's often required for:

  • Opening bank accounts
  • Applying for loans
  • Signing contracts
  • KRA registration
  • Due diligence by partners

How to get CR12: Login to eCitizen, go to Business Registration Service, select "Order CR12", pay KES 1,000, and download instantly.

Foreign Company Registration

Foreign companies can register in Kenya in two ways:

1. Register a Local Subsidiary

Same process as local companies but with additional requirements:

  • At least one local director (can be foreigner with work permit)
  • Registered office in Kenya
  • Foreign directors' passport copies and proof of residence

2. Register a Branch Office

For foreign companies wanting to operate directly in Kenya:

  • Certificate of Incorporation from home country (apostilled)
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association
  • List of directors of parent company
  • Local representative appointed
  • Registration fee: KES 20,000

⚠️ Important for Foreign Investors

Foreign investors require a work permit (Class D or G) to be directors. Consider using a local nominee director while permits are being processed.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Incorrect Share Structure

Setting share distribution that doesn't reflect actual ownership. This can cause problems during investor due diligence.

❌ Wrong Company Type

Registering as a sole proprietorship when you need limited liability protection.

❌ Using Personal Address

Using a residential address as registered office without landlord's consent.

❌ Missing Post-Registration Steps

Forgetting to register for KRA PIN, apply for permits, or open bank account.

❌ Not Filing Annual Returns

Companies must file annual returns with BRS within 30 days of anniversary. Penalty: KES 2,000 per month late.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does company registration take?

▼

Typically 3-5 working days if all documents are correct. Same-day service available for KES 5,000 extra.

Can a foreigner register a company in Kenya?

▼

Yes, foreigners can register companies. They need a valid passport and may require a work permit to act as director.

What is the minimum share capital?

▼

There's no minimum share capital requirement. You can start with as little as KES 1,000 in share capital.

Can I register a company online?

▼

Yes, the entire process is online through the eCitizen portal. No need to visit any physical office.

Do I need a physical office to register?

▼

Yes, you need a registered physical address in Kenya. This can be a virtual office or co-working space.

What is the difference between LTD and LTD (PLC)?

▼

LTD is a private company (shares not publicly traded). PLC is a public company whose shares can be traded on the stock exchange.
