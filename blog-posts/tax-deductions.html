Introduction

Did you know that many Kenyan businesses pay more tax than necessary simply because they don't claim all the deductions they're entitled to? Understanding what expenses you can deduct from your taxable income is crucial for minimizing your tax liability and keeping more of your hard-earned money.

This comprehensive guide covers all the tax deductions available to Kenyan businesses, from everyday operating expenses to capital allowances and industry-specific deductions. We'll also highlight common pitfalls to avoid and provide practical examples to help you maximize your tax savings.

📌 Key Principle

For an expense to be deductible, it must be "wholly and exclusively" incurred in the production of income. It cannot be capital in nature (except through capital allowances) and must be properly documented.

Operating Expenses

These are day-to-day expenses that are fully deductible in the year they are incurred:

🏢

Rent & Utilities

Fully Allowable

Rent for business premises, electricity, water, internet, and security services.

Documentation
Lease Agreement, Receipts
Limits
None
Example
KES 100,000/mo rent
👥

Salaries & Wages

Fully Allowable

Employee compensation including salaries, wages, bonuses, commissions, and allowances (provided PAYE is remitted).

Documentation
Payroll records, PAYE receipts
Limits
Must be reasonable
Example
KES 500,000 monthly payroll
📞

Office & Administrative

Fully Allowable

Office supplies, stationery, printing, postage, telephone, and communication expenses.

Documentation
Invoices, receipts
Limits
Business use only
Example
KES 20,000/mo office supplies
🚗

Travel & Transport

Fully Allowable

Business travel expenses including fuel, maintenance, public transport, and accommodation for business trips.

Documentation
Travel logs, receipts
Limits
Business purpose only
Example
KES 50,000/mo travel
📊

Professional Fees

Fully Allowable

Legal fees, accounting fees, audit fees, consultancy fees, and other professional services.

Documentation
Invoices, contracts
Limits
Must be business-related
Example
KES 30,000/mo accounting fees
📈

Marketing & Advertising

Fully Allowable

Advertising costs, marketing campaigns, website development, and promotional materials.

Documentation
Contracts, receipts
Limits
Must be reasonable
Example
KES 100,000 campaign cost

Capital Allowances (Depreciation)

While capital expenditure is not directly deductible, you can claim capital allowances (depreciation) on qualifying assets:

Asset Class Rate Examples
Industrial Buildings 10% (straight line) Factories, warehouses, workshops
Hotels 10% (straight line) Hotels, lodges, restaurants
Machinery & Equipment 12.5% (reducing balance) Manufacturing equipment, generators
Computers & Software 20% (reducing balance) Computers, servers, software
Motor Vehicles 25% (reducing balance) Commercial vehicles, pickup trucks
Furniture & Fittings 12.5% (reducing balance) Office furniture, shelves, counters
Farm Works 20% (straight line) Farm buildings, fences, water systems
Telecom Equipment 20% (reducing balance) Communication equipment

📝 Example: Capital Allowance Calculation

Asset: Delivery Vehicle costing KES 2,000,000
Rate: 25% reducing balance

Year 1: KES 2,000,000 × 25% = KES 500,000 allowance
Tax written down value: KES 1,500,000

Year 2: KES 1,500,000 × 25% = KES 375,000 allowance
Tax written down value: KES 1,125,000

Investment Deductions

Some assets qualify for accelerated deductions:

  • Manufacturing: 100% deduction in first year for qualifying investments
  • EPZ: 100% investment deduction
  • Shipping: 100% for ships over 125 tons

Specific Deductions

💳

Interest Expense

Allowable

Interest on loans used for business purposes is deductible, subject to thin capitalization rules.

Limit: Interest on loans from related parties is limited to 30% of EBITDA (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization).

💧

Insurance Premiums

Allowable

Business insurance premiums including fire, theft, professional indemnity, and group life for employees.

📚

Training & Education

Allowable

Staff training costs, seminars, workshops, and professional development courses.

🔧

Repairs & Maintenance

Allowable

Costs to maintain assets in working condition. Note: Improvements that extend asset life are capital in nature.

📦

Bad Debts

Allowable

Specific debts written off as bad, provided all reasonable steps were taken to recover them.

Note: General provisions are NOT deductible. Only specific bad debts qualify.

📑

Pension Contributions

Allowable

Employer contributions to registered pension schemes are deductible up to certain limits.

Non-Allowable Deductions

❌ These expenses CANNOT be deducted:

  • Capital expenditure - Asset purchases (except through capital allowances)
  • Personal expenses - Owner's personal costs, family expenses
  • Income tax - Corporate tax, PAYE, VAT (these are not business expenses)
  • Fines & penalties - KRA penalties, traffic fines, court penalties
  • Donations - Unless to approved charitable organizations
  • Depreciation - Accounting depreciation (use capital allowances instead)
  • Goodwill - Amortization of goodwill
  • Club subscriptions - Memberships to clubs and associations
  • Entertainment - Client entertainment (staff entertainment 50% deductible)
  • Political contributions - Donations to political parties or candidates
  • Reserves - General provisions and reserves
  • Dividends - Payments to shareholders

⚠️ Partial Restrictions

Entertainment: Staff entertainment is 50% deductible. Client entertainment is 0% deductible.
Passenger vehicles: Capital allowances limited to vehicles costing up to KES 3 million.

Personal Relief

👤

Personal Relief

KES 2,400/month

Every resident individual is entitled to a personal relief of KES 2,400 per month (KES 28,800 per year). This is deducted from the tax calculated on your income.

How it works:

Tax on taxable income: KES 50,000
Less Personal Relief: KES 2,400
Net Tax Payable: KES 47,600

👴

Insurance Relief

15% of premiums

15% relief on life insurance premiums and education policies, up to a maximum of KES 60,000 per year.

🏠

Pension/Retirement Relief

Up to KES 20,000

Contributions to registered pension schemes and provident funds are deductible up to KES 20,000 per month (KES 240,000 per year).

🏥

Health Insurance Relief

15% of premiums

15% relief on health insurance premiums for yourself and dependents, up to specified limits.

Losses

Loss Carry Forward

If your business makes a tax loss in a year, you can carry that loss forward to offset future profits for up to 9 years (5 years for some sectors).

📝 Example: Loss Utilization

Year 1: Loss of KES 500,000
Year 2: Profit of KES 800,000
Taxable profit: KES 800,000 - KES 500,000 = KES 300,000
Tax saved: KES 500,000 × 30% = KES 150,000

⚠️ Important

Losses can only be carried forward if you continue to carry on the same business. A change in business ownership may affect loss utilization.

Donations & Charitable Contributions

❤️

Charitable Donations

Restricted

Donations to approved charitable organizations are deductible, subject to limits:

  • Cash donations to registered NGOs with charitable status
  • Donations to hospitals, educational institutions, and religious organizations
  • Limited to a percentage of taxable income
  • Must have official receipts

Limit: Generally limited to 5% of taxable income for companies, with variations for specific causes.

🎓

Educational Donations

Special Rules

Donations to educational institutions for research, scholarships, or infrastructure may qualify for higher deductions under specific regulations.

Industry-Specific Deductions

🌾

Agriculture

  • Farm works allowance (20% straight line)
  • Farm irrigation equipment (100% first year)
  • Dairy and livestock expenses
  • Seeds, fertilizers, and farm chemicals
  • Veterinary costs
🏭

Manufacturing

  • 100% investment deduction for qualifying assets
  • Factory building allowance (10% straight line)
  • Industrial building deduction
  • Research and development costs
🚢

Shipping & Transport

  • 100% deduction for ships over 125 tons
  • Commercial vehicle allowance (25% reducing balance)
  • Fuel and maintenance costs
  • Licenses and permits
🏨

Tourism & Hospitality

  • Hotel building allowance (10% straight line)
  • Tourism promotion expenses
  • Classification and licensing fees
  • Staff training in hospitality

Documentation Requirements

To claim any deduction, you must have proper documentation:

✓

Original Invoices

With supplier's KRA PIN, date, description, and amount

✓

Official Receipts

For all payments made

✓

Contracts/Agreements

For services, leases, and major transactions

✓

Bank Statements

Showing proof of payment

✓

Asset Register

For capital allowance claims

⚠️ Retention Period

All supporting documents must be kept for 5 years from the date of filing the return. KRA can request these during audits.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mixing Personal and Business Expenses

Using business funds for personal expenses and claiming them as deductions. Keep separate accounts.

❌ Insufficient Documentation

Claiming expenses without proper receipts or invoices. KRA will disallow these in an audit.

❌ Missing Capital Allowances

Forgetting to claim depreciation on assets, leaving money on the table.

❌ Claiming Non-Allowable Items

Claiming entertainment, fines, or capital expenditure as regular expenses.

❌ Not Carrying Forward Losses

Failing to track and utilize losses in future profitable years.

❌ Incorrect Asset Classification

Applying wrong capital allowance rates to assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I deduct my home office expenses?

▼

Yes, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, and other home expenses proportional to the space used exclusively for business.

What if I don't have receipts for small expenses?

▼

KRA requires documentation for all deductions. Without receipts, expenses may be disqualified during an audit.

Can I deduct interest on my business loan?

▼

Yes, interest on loans used wholly for business is deductible, subject to thin capitalization rules.

How long can I carry forward losses?

▼

Tax losses can be carried forward for up to 9 years (5 years for some sectors).

Are directors' fees deductible?

▼

Yes, directors' fees are deductible as long as they are reasonable and PAYE is remitted where applicable.

Can I deduct VAT on expenses?

▼

No, VAT is not deductible for income tax purposes. However, you may claim it as input VAT in your VAT return.
